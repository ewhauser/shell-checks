#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/30480116?v=4" width="50">[Davoodeh](https://github.com/Davoodeh) commented at [2020-07-17T09:32:12Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/917#issuecomment-659994990):

I found a related instance that I'd like to share here:
```sh
#!/bin/sh
magic=var
var=value
[ -n "${!magic}" ] && do something
       ^------^ SC2039: In POSIX sh, indirect expansion is undefined.
```
Throws the error, but there is not much of explaination related to it (which is fine but not so noob friendly).

In case you are here to find the answer: you can fix it by replacing `"${!magic}"` with `"$(eval printf "%s" "\$$magic")"` (prints "$var" and gets the "value")
