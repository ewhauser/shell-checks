# [\#621 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/621) `closed`: SC1037 should give a non-POSIX warning; also it's invalid for dash

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/6459309?v=4" width="50">[Artoria2e5](https://github.com/Artoria2e5) opened issue at [2016-03-01T15:58:43Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/621):

Per https://gnu.org/s/autoconf/manual/html_node/Shell-Substitutions.html, section '`${10}`':
- Any attempts to access positional params beyond `$9` is non-POSIX. This matches my memory of POSIX V3 Chap2, but I am too lazy to look at that now. May want to add SC2039 too.
- `dash` [wrongly](https://gnu.org/s/autoconf/manual/html_node/Shell-Substitutions.html) interprets `$10` as `${10}` by default (and there are no switches available). ~~Since it's a rather popular shell, and often thought to be a very POSIX one, perhaps it's good to warn about the non-deterministic-by-mistake property of the construct too.~~

(Yeah, autoconf 'portable shell scripting' is so damn good for showing how almost every shell sucks…)

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/1771172?v=4" width="50">[EliahKagan](https://github.com/EliahKagan) commented at [2019-07-30T05:38:39Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/621#issuecomment-516270290):

As [mentioned](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/621#issuecomment-194055788), POSIX supports expanding positional parameters past `9` (and has at least since [SUSv2](http://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/7990989775/xcu/chap2.html#tag_001_005_001)), so [I've removed](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC1037/_compare/a46e8c0fde97716cf224d25df4bdb53b79c98ad5...454dfb5980960606da01e060cefcb3d6f547ba76) the "non-POSIX" note from the [SC1037 wiki page](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC1037). I hope that's okay.
