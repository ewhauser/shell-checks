SC2029 is reported for this line:

```
ssh -f -N -R 60000:localhost:60000 "user@$ip"
```

I suppose SC considers `"user@$ip"` something that's executed on the remote server, but it's not.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/197253?v=4" width="50">[kurahaupo](https://github.com/kurahaupo) commented at [2016-07-14T00:32:44Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/327#issuecomment-232525506):

On Wed, 13 Jul 2016, hpollinger wrote:

> ```
> ssh "root@$user" "$scriptdir/script.sh $scriptargs"
> ```

Surely that's root@$host rather than root@$user?

I would strongly recommend that for scripting one should use

```
ssh -l "$user" "$host" ...
```

Or even put "User remoteusername" in your .ssh/config under the appropriate
"Host" entry.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/10137?v=4" width="50">[ghost](https://github.com/ghost) commented at [2016-12-13T12:46:10Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/327#issuecomment-266729617):

Found another case when checking my script.

I was doing `ssh -o BatchMode=yes "$host"` and it kept telling me that `$host` is expanded on the clientside, which is quite obvious.

Quick testing showed that same happens with ssh options that take an argument. Shellcheck seems ton interpret the argument as the host.

I tried with `-L 1234:somehost:1234` and `-D 1234` and both will complain about this same thing.

Also adding such options after the host will also cause the error to trigger about their options. `ssh "$host" -D "$port"` triggers SC2029 for `$port`.
