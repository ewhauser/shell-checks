```sh

#!/bin/bash
echo a=b=c | tr = ,

```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:
```sh
Line 2:
echo a=b=c | tr = ,
             ^-- SC2034: tr appears unused. Verify use (or export if used externally).
                ^-- SC1068: Don't put spaces around the = in assignments (or quote to make it literal).
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:
```sh
Line 2:
echo a=b=c | tr = ,
                ^-- SC1068: Don't put spaces around the = in assignments (or quote to make it literal).
```

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2020-08-24 01:51](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2022#issuecomment-678863449):

ShellCheck now parses assignments according to spec without leniency for spaces around the `=` or for `$` on the left-hand side, and therefore no longer sees this as an assignment (SC1068 is still emitted). Thanks!
