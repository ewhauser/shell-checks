#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2024-02-26 02:59](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2737#issuecomment-1963234094):

`-exec ... +` is actually a POSIX standard, not a GNU specific feature. I definitely feel your pain trying to write AIX compatible scripts and I would love to be able to check against e.g. AT&T UNIX System 3 compatibility, but unfortunately this is way too granular, so POSIX compatibility is the lowest baseline available at this time.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/1413391?u=f157dd3a9aaac55220670c45f91670f904ff9a90&v=4" width="50">[bmarwell](https://github.com/bmarwell) commented at [2024-02-26 19:10](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2737#issuecomment-1965003235):

Thanks, found it:

> `-exec  utility_name  [argument ...] ;`
> `-exec  utility_name  [argument ...]   {} +`
>    The end of the primary expression shall be punctuated by a semicolon or by a plus sign.

source: https://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/007904975/utilities/find.html
