#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/197253?v=4" width="50">[kurahaupo](https://github.com/kurahaupo) commented at [2015-09-08T12:56:28Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/456#issuecomment-138551909):

Presumably you mean other than inside a function?

Note that `mysource() { . "$@" ; } ; mysource foo.bash` will permit`local` outside a function within `foo.bash`.

Some shells actually permit `local` at the top level of any sourced file, without needing an additional function context. For those shells it is not a mistake. Indeed, I would consider it a worthwhile addition to Bash.
