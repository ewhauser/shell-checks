#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh

while read -r _spacecheck; do
	case ${_spacecheck} in
	line*:*)
	        echo "Match asterisk"
	        ;;
	*[[:space:]]:*)
                echo "Match character class"
		;;
	*)
		echo "No match"
	esac
done <<-EOT
	line one: no space
        line two : one space
        line three  : two spaces
EOT
```

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/19507282?v=4" width="50">[automorphism88](https://github.com/automorphism88) commented at [2019-12-24T17:47:11Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1753#issuecomment-568784323):

Looks like your first pattern will match all three lines in your heredoc, so the second one is irrelevant and never gets evaluated. Why not test with just the one case pattern that's at issue? Another issue with your test script is that `read` is going to eliminate those extra spaces unless you add `IFS=` between while and read to prevent word splitting. Add an `echo "${_spacecheck}"` above the case statement and you'll see.

Maybe tell us what you expected the script's output to be, and what you got instead that makes you think shellcheck should flag it? Or if it's a bash vs sh issue, have you tested it in bash? And what is your default shell? It looks to me like POSIX also specifies character classes are allowed in case patterns (https://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/009695399/utilities/xcu_chap02.html), and if your shell is not POSIX sh compliant that's a bug in the shell, not shellcheck.
