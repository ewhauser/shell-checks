#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh
export var=$'\033[0m'
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

```console
export VAR=$'\e[1;32m'
           ^-- [SC3003](https://www.shellcheck.net/wiki/SC3003) (warning): In POSIX sh, $'..' is undefined.
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Believe this could be a false positive. [POSIX.1-2024](https://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/9799919799/) seems to have added this, under [§2.2.4 Dollar-Single-Quotes](https://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/9799919799/utilities/V3_chap02.html#tag_19_02_04).

Can be found at Austin Group's Bug Tracker: [0000249: Add standard support for $'...' in shell](https://austingroupbugs.net/view.php?id=249).

Ideally, the current warning should disappear for POSIX shells. Its functionality could be preserved in an optional flag to work with legacy shells.
