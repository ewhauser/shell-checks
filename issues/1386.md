#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-11-25T20:19:07Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1386#issuecomment-441469589):

This is not related to the space, and you'll see that the warning does not go away if you remove it. Your interpreter is `/usr/bin/env` and you are passing two parameters: `bash` and `-u`. Most OS do not allow this. For example, here's what happens on Linux:

```
$ cat foo
#! /usr/bin/env bash -u
echo "hello world"

$ ./foo
/usr/bin/env: ‘bash -u’: No such file or directory
```
