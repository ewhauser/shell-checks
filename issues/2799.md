#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh

#!/bin/bash

foo="foo Ready bar"
if [[ $foo == "*Ready*" ]]; then
    echo "Ready"
fi

```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

no issues found


#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

I would like to get a warning, since this does not work as expected. You need `*Ready*` for pattern matching (without quotes).


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/197253?v=4" width="50">[kurahaupo](https://github.com/kurahaupo) commented at [2023-07-21 12:00](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2799#issuecomment-1645470584):

How is ShellCheck supposed to guess that you didn't want to check for the literal text `*Ready*`? (Perhaps you're checking a variable that holds patterns to see if it contains a particular pattern?)

You would expect `rm "*.txt"` to remove ONE (oddly named) file, so why *wouldn't* you expect `[[ $x = "*.txt" ]]` to tell you whether `x` contains exactly that filename?

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/197253?v=4" width="50">[kurahaupo](https://github.com/kurahaupo) commented at [2023-07-21 12:18](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2799#issuecomment-1645493274):

We don't need ShellCheck to warn us about things that are *always* broken, such as extra quotes, since those will be obvious during testing. We do need ShellCheck to warn us about things that are *sometimes* broken, such as missing quotes.
