# [\#436 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/436) `closed`: Warn about starting a bracket expression with an unquoted ^

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/9367?v=4" width="50">[kergoth](https://github.com/kergoth) opened issue at [2015-08-12T17:54:17Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/436):

According to the standard:

"[...]a bracket expression as in XBD RE Bracket Expression , except that the <exclamation-mark> character ( '!' ) shall replace the <circumflex> character ( '^' ) in its role in a non-matching list in the regular expression notation"

Most shells support both (e.g. `[!a-z]` and `[^a-z]`), but the latter gives unspecified results according to the standard:

"A bracket expression starting with an unquoted `<circumflex>` character produces unspecified results."

I was recently bitten by this with a couple of my shell scripts on a dash system, and also on an embedded system with a feature-limited /bin/sh.
