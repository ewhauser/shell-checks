## Iterating over ls output is fragile. Use globs.

### Problematic code:

    for f in $(ls *.wav)
    do
      echo "$f"
    done

### Correct code:

    for f in *.wav
    do
      [[ -e $f ]] || break  # handle the case of no *.wav files
      echo "$f"
    done

### how about "ls -A", also can use "for f in *.wav"?

    for f in $(ls -A *.wav)
    do
      echo "$f"
    done

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-11-01T12:02:43Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1375#issuecomment-435019922):

To loop over all files including hidden files, use `for f in .[^.]* *; do ..` or (in bash) `shopt -s dotglob; for f in *; do ..`

For loops over `ls -A` output is problematic, just like `ls` output.
