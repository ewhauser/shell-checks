This may be related to #790 .

#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/bash
s='*'
a=($s)
printf '%s\n' "${a[@]}"
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

    No issues detected!

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

```
Line 3:
a=($s)
   ^-- SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.
```

Since in such an assignment, globbing and word splitting may happen, I'd expect shellcheck to invoke SC2086.  I suspect that what is happening is that it is treating this assignment like a non-array assignment (eg, `foo=$bar`), in which the globbing and word splitting wouldn't happen.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2017-04-02T17:04:49Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/864#issuecomment-290999406):

The root cause is actually intentional; it doesn't trigger for the same reason `for var in $string` doesn't.

I agree though. There should be a warning, even though this is a common way to intentionally split things.  Maybe a separate message that's more helpful in the array case.
