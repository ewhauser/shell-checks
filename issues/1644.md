#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-10-14T17:42:22Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1644#issuecomment-943578681):

Missed this one, but I'm happy you're finding it useful! This was indeed a new feature in 0.7.2 where a leading `$x/` or `$(x)/` is treated like `./` for the purpose of looking for the file. In 0.7.0, ShellCheck unconditionally bailed when the source path was not a literal string, causing the error you see.
