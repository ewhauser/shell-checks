#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2199511?u=6cc22d0767078b50e47021699ffbb376251628ad&v=4" width="50">[Nightfirecat](https://github.com/Nightfirecat) commented at [2020-02-13 19:34](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1836#issuecomment-585934302):

Looks like shellcheck is incorrectly assuming that `$((` here denotes the start of an arithmetic operation.

As an aside, is your snippet not the same as the following?

```sh
#!/bin/bash
_delete=$(/usr/bin/sshpass -p $_password sftp $_username@$_server <<< "$_command")
```

If so, this would help you avoid the false positive SC1106 in your script for now.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2020-02-17 20:28](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1836#issuecomment-587147305):

There's a more informative message being suppressed:

        ^-- SC1102: Shells disambiguate $(( differently or not at all. For $(command substition), add space after $( . For $((arithmetics)), fix parsing errors.

I just went with the pragmatic workaround of bumping that from a warning to an error so it's easier to see why ShellCheck is interpreting this incorrectly and what to do about it.

Obviously correct code shouldn't trigger and it would be simple enough to throw away any errors relating to arithmetic expressions in this kind of construct, but this would again complicate things for people with the opposite problem of trying to figure out why it's *not* being treated as a `$((..))`
