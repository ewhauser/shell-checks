This issue is specific to one Bash project that I maintain called [SteamTinkerLaunch](https://github.com/sonic2kk/steamtinkerlaunch). With shellcheck 0.8.0, there were no issues. When I updated to shellcheck 0.9.0, I started encountering a problem. 

Running shellcheck v.0.9.0 on SteamTinkerLaunch (a 22,000+ lines Bash script), shellcheck will keep using ram until a segfault or a system crash. I am running a PC with 32GB RAM and before a system lockup, KSysGuard said 30GB RAM was being used.

I have tested shelllcheck from the [official Arch repos](https://archlinux.org/packages/community/x86_64/shellcheck/) (0.9.0-3 at time of writing), and the [GitHub 0.9.0 release](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/releases/tag/v0.9.0).

I have confirmed that with [shellcheck 0.8.0](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/releases/tag/v0.8.0), this issue is not present. I am currently using 0.8.0 to check SteamTinkerLaunch.

I have a couple of very small test scripts that I used to prototype ideas, there are only about 20 lines long or so. I tested shellcheck 0.8.0 and 0.9.0 on these scripts and they both worked without issue (in fact, 0.9.0 seemed marginally faster). These scripts don't have any fancy code, I just tested on them to see if they would also reproduce the issue and they did not.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2023-01-02 05:09](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2652#issuecomment-1368662822):

This is undoubtedly due to ShellCheck's new data flow analysis engine. It takes great care to be acceptably fast even for larger scripts, but 22,000 lines is something else. Given the amount of optimization work that went into it in the first place, I'm guessing the most realistic solution is a flag that disables DFA and either skips those checks or reverts to the old, naive algorithm.
