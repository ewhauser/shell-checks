#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-07-05T03:00:49Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1269#issuecomment-402593646):

The online version has some resource constraints to avoid accidental DoS from especially large or complex scripts. Up until last week, exceeding them accidentally caused a hang, but now you get an explicit message as you've seen.

I never really expected people to regularly use it on 1000+ line scripts, it was mostly meant for demo purposes. The limits haven't changed in a while, but ram usage tends to creep up as functionality grows.

There is a package available for Raspbian (stretch at least), and I'm working on official builds. I'll also look into better ways of hosting the online version.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-09-27T17:22:28Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1269#issuecomment-425174535):

@sbf- This happens on versions before 0.4.6 when your system locale doesn't match the script's encoding. 

The best option might be to get a more up to date version. ShellCheck's own statically linked Raspberry Pi binary is linked to from the readme: https://storage.googleapis.com/shellcheck/shellcheck-stable.linux.armv6hf.tar.xz 

Alternatively, you can keep using the older version by specifying a locale that your script is compatible with, e.g. `LC_ALL=en_US.iso88591 shellcheck yourfile` (see `locale -a` for a list of available locales). Or determine what the discrepancy and fix your file or environment accordingly.
