# [\#210 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/210) `closed`: Stricter POSIX compliance for "sh" scripts

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/5316?v=4" width="50">[mcandre](https://github.com/mcandre) opened issue at [2014-08-08T17:58:25Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/210):

Could we have an optional `--posix-compliant` rule that turns on additional checks for "sh" scripts?

`sh` may default to `bash` on many Linux systems, but on Unixes, it may default to other shells, or even the ancient POSIX `sh`. So a POSIX compliance rule could help these "sh" scripts be more cross-platform compatible.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2014-08-09T04:37:54Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/210#issuecomment-51677023):

POSIX mode is enabled by specifying `#!/bin/sh` or with `-s sh`.
