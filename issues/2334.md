#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-09-26 03:27](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2334#issuecomment-927222553):

The suggestions do not correspond to the code, so I'll assume you meant that this is the current output:

```
        declare -g "$varname"="${!varname}
                             ^-- SC2140: Word is of the form "A"B"C" (B indicated). Did you mean "ABC" or "A\"B\"C"?
```

ShellCheck is right in that the inner quotes don't serve a purpose, and that `declare -g "$varname=${!varname}..` would do the same thing but shorter. However, to a human, it's clearly not unintentional and instead a stylistic choice.

The suggestion has been suppressed when the trapped string is `=`, `/` or `:` which appeared to be the most common cases. Thanks!
