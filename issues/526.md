# [\#526 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/526) `closed`: SC2005 false positive on `echo "$(<1.txt)"`, and new bashism.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/6459309?v=4" width="50">[Artoria2e5](https://github.com/Artoria2e5) opened issue at [2015-10-28T05:06:05Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/526):

This is a bashism.

In the bash manpage, `"$(<1.txt)"` is said to be faster than `"$(cat 1.txt)"` if you only need one file. From that, people naturally gets `echo "$(<1.txt)"` (a second thought may let people switch to `printf %s\\n` though).
