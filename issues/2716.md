#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh

#!/bin/sh

packages="one two three four"

check_packages() {
    for pkg; do
        echo "checking $pkg..."
    done
}

check_packages $packages

```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

```
Line 11:
check_packages $packages
               ^-- SC2086 (info): Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.

Did you mean:
check_packages "$packages"
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

The help on the wiki for POSIX sh suggests creating a function which works fine if you know the list of function arguments ahead of time (e.g. `check_packages one two three four`). In my case I don't know ahead of time what `$packages` will be. The wiki then goes on to say:

> To split on spaces but not perform glob expansion, POSIX has a `set -f` to disable globbing. You can disable word splitting by setting `IFS=''`.

I _think_ this (split on spaces without globbing) is what I want for my case but it's not clear to me how to put this suggestion into practice.

In short, I am trying to accomplish the above without disabling the shellcheck warning and without using arrays.
