#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh

CFLAGS=-O2
cc -o foo foo.c
cc -o bar bar.c
```

#### Here's what `shellcheck` currently says:
```log
Line 3:
CFLAGS=-O2
^-- SC2034 (warning): CFLAGS appears unused. Verify use (or export if used externally).
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

No warnings, `gcc`/`clang` implicitly use this variable.

Would it make sense that `shellcheck` whitelists `CFLAGS` and related variables?
There are other similar, common ones, present in [POSIX 2017.1: _Vol 1. Base Definitions_, _§8. Environment Variables_](https://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/9699919799/basedefs/V1_chap08.html)
