#### The problem:

When trying to quickly determine whether there is any problem with any of a possibly large amount of files it would be very useful if `shellcheck` could

1. print nothing (or at least not to standard output) and
1. `exit 1` as soon as any issue is detected.

This would be equivalent to grep's `--quiet`, which also prints nothing and exits as soon as a match is found.
