#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-08-30 17:02](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2311#issuecomment-908515183):

I had a look and found a few cases of this (mostly `echo` in fact), but I was unable to find a nice general pattern with a high signal:noise ratio. Common false positives were indirections like `run"$1"`, and scripts with mismatched quotes where `echo "foo"` was actually `"echo "foo"..."` and already detected. Several of the true positives like `echo"hello."` were also already detected by the more general "command ends with dot" checks. I'll leave this one to the shell's fairly clear error message. 

Thanks for the suggestion!
