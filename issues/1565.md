#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```bash
#!/usr/bin/env bash

declare REPODIR="/tmp/zz"

declare -a DIRELMTS=("${REPODIR}"/* "${REPODIR}"/.*)

for EL in "${DIRELMTS[@]}"; do
   if ! [[ "${EL}" =~ "${REPODIR}/"("."|".."|"somedir"|".git")$ ]]; then
       echo rm -rf "${EL}"
   fi
done

```

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2019-05-04T19:20:37Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1565#issuecomment-489357398):

You're right of course, this has now been fixed. ShellCheck parses the `()|`s in `=~` regex as simple literals even though they're not, and that caused this warning to overtrigger. Thanks!
