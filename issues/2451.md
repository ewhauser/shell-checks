#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2022-02-20 20:10](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2451#issuecomment-1046311592):

In general, you definitely have to quote $(..) inside $(..), so the warning is not wrong. In this specific case, Linux (procps-ng) `ps` outputs the value padded with spaces, which therefore can't be reused as input, making it more awkward than normal.

You were relying on word splitting to remove these spaces, which is realistically ok in this specific case, though you can also consider `$((..))` as suggested here, or simply use `$PPID`. 

There's also `ppid:1=` to avoid padding the field so that plain quoting works, but this is Linux-specific and will e.g. fail on macOS where `ps` does not output the padding in the first place anyways.
