#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2020-12-02 06:17](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2096#issuecomment-737018144):

$((..)) always expands to a number, so (unless you set `IFS=5`) it doesn't need quoting. You also don't need to quote in arithmetic contexts, and from my reading of POSIX, bash allowing it at all is an extension.

(There's an issue about actively warning about quotes in `$((..))` for sh/dash somewhere.)
