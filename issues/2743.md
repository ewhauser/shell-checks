#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/197253?v=4" width="50">[kurahaupo](https://github.com/kurahaupo) commented at [2023-07-31 04:42](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2743#issuecomment-1657564644):

Using `some_command $(pidof ...)` warrants its own warning, as it introduces a race condition, and always runs `some_command` even if no processes are identified.

Using `pidof` should be a last resort for managing processes.

1. Use your system process manager (systemd, upstart, init, rc, etc)
2. Actively manage your processes: in your "start" scripts, store the process IDs of started jobs; then in your "stop" scripts, retrieve them. (If one script invocation does both, it can use variables; otherwise use files.)
3. To kill unmanaged processes, use `pkill ...` (or its equivalent on your system)
4.  Avoid `kill $(pidof ...)` if at all possible.
