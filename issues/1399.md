Nothing. There is no error in there. ShellCheck mistakenly checks for echo flags inside the string.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-11-25T20:12:17Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1399#issuecomment-441469034):

Since they are interpreted by the command, there is no difference between a flag and a string. `echo -n` and `echo '-n'` are the same thing.

No implementation of `echo` that I'm aware of will do anything other than write this giant flag/string verbatim, but I'd still argue in favor of `printf`
