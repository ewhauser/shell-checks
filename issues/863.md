#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/bash
cd /foo
pushd /bar
popd
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

```
In demo.sh line 2:
cd /foo
^-- SC2164: Use 'cd ... || exit' or 'cd ... || return' in case cd fails.
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

```
In demo.sh line 2:
cd /foo
^-- SC2164: Use 'cd ... || exit' or 'cd ... || return' in case cd fails.


In demo.sh line 3:
pushd /bar
^-- SC2164: Use 'pushd ... || exit' or 'pushd ... || return' in case pushd fails.


In demo.sh line 4:
popd
^-- SC2164: Use 'popd || exit' or 'popd || return' in case popd fails.
```
`pushd` and `popd` have exactly the same failure cases as `cd`; SC2164 should apply to them as well.
