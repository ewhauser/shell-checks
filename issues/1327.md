#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-09-03T18:59:37Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1327#issuecomment-418177831):

ShellCheck is right. You should be using `-n` and you should be quoting it.

`[ ! -z ${var+x} ]` works mostly by accident because when the variable is unset, it becomes `[ ! -z ]` which is equivalent to `[ ! -n "-z" ]` which is false, and that happens to be the expected result. It's clever but it's not good practice.
