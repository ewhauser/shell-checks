#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/102495?v=4" width="50">[ssbarnea](https://github.com/ssbarnea) opened issue at [2018-07-27T16:55:15Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1306):

More and more shell scripts are migrated from pure bash to being jinja2 templates as they are
easily process by ansible.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/40320097?v=4" width="50">[lonix1](https://github.com/lonix1) commented at [2022-06-07T01:44:54Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1306#issuecomment-1148103632):

The fix is to have a disable-then-enable directive as I proposed in #2512.

That is because shellcheck chokes on `{{` and `}}` (and related brace syntax) - **but everything else works**. So if we could wrap those lines in "disable then enable" directives, everything would work.

(And for any pesky errors/warnings, we could use a normal disable directive, as usual.)

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2920223?v=4" width="50">[schewara](https://github.com/schewara) commented at [2024-10-05T10:19:15Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1306#issuecomment-2395008466):

Just stumbling into this problem myself and with some spelunking around, I discovered

- [jinjanator](https://github.com/kpfleming/jinjanator)

which looks like a promising tool to integrate or hook into shellcheck.

As Jinja is just one of many [template engines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comparison_of_web_template_engines) it might be overkill for this project to support most of them.

```
$ jinjanate test.sh.j2 testdata.yml | shellcheck -
```
should work without any big issues.

#2512 in addition also looks like a good alternative approach, which reminds me of Jinjas own [*raw* blocks](https://jinja.palletsprojects.com/en/3.0.x/templates/#escaping) approach.
