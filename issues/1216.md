This is a tricky one. I understand the need for this warning, but I can't find a way to sensibly `grep` for a real asterisk without having to explicitly silence the warning.

#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh
grep \* /etc/passwd
```

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/8651654?v=4" width="50">[jleffler](https://github.com/jleffler) commented at [2018-05-12T06:42:35Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1216#issuecomment-388534654):

Formally, you should make sure `grep` sees a backslash and an asterisk: `grep '\*' /etc/passwd`.

You get away with `grep \* /etc/passwd` because `grep` recognizes that `*` on its own can only match `*` because there's nothing for it to repeat zero or more times.
