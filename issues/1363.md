#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh

var="$(head -c 4 "/dev/urandom")"

printf "${var}"
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:
Exceptions

Sometimes you may actually want to interpret data as a format string, like in:

hexToAscii() { printf "\x$1"; }
hexToAscii 21

or when you have a pattern in a variable:

filepattern="file-%d.jpg"
printf -v filename "$filepattern" "$number"

These are valid use cases with no useful rewrites. Please ignore the warnings with a directive.
#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:
Of course, I could fully missunderstand (or it not being fully posix compliant) but isn't one way to handle the exception to printf with %b?

e.g. `printf "%b" "\x$1"`

It seems to work in busybox's ash, (but not dash, though dash doesn't even accept "\x$1".

While the directive is one way to solve this, I suppose it should at least mentioned on the wiki that "%b" is acceptable (not acceptable) in certain cases and may be preferred over a directive?

Note, that shellcheck is completly happy with printf "%b" "\x$1" so if it is not acceptable or should be avoided, then there's a missing check.
