- Rule Id: `SC2086`
- My shellcheck version: `online`
- [x] I read the issue's wiki page, e.g. https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC2086

#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh

#!/bin/sh

set -- 1 2 3
arg=$#

echo $arg

```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:

```
Line 6:
echo $arg
     ^-- SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.
```

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Nothing.

My understanding is that `$arg` contains the number of arguments (`$#`) and will always be a number that will not suffer globbing or word splitting. Is this correct?

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-10-18T03:11:46Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1362#issuecomment-430860741):

Well caught. `$#` is indeed always a number, and will be counted as such for SC2086 from now on.
