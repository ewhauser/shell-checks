#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-05-27T05:02:15Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1238#issuecomment-392305491):

You're right, of course.

ShellCheck's design decision is to generally avoid introspection of even trivial forms of indirect evaluation, to avoid giving the impression that this should work more generally. In this case, it contradicts that enough to look for `$1` in aliases, but not enough to actually find the context, thereby resulting in an annoying false positive.

Here's the wiki's suggestion:

>If your alias ends up quoting the value, e.g. `alias cut_first="awk '{print \$1}'"`, you can technically ignore this error. However, you should consider turning this alias into a more readable function instead: 
`cut_first() { awk '{print $1}' "$@"; }`

I'm not sure if it would be better to blacklist any alias that contains the word `awk`, try to parse `alias` commands as stand-alone function bodies, try to parse the alias in the context where it's used (like a shell), or just suggest functions for any use of aliases. They all have their cons.

It's infrequent enough that I'll leave it for another time. Meanwhile, consider a function. Unlike the wiki's dubious example, it actually *would* be more readable :P
