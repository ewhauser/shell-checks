#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/bin/sh

fswatch . --recursive --utc-time --one-per-batch --insensitive --allow-overflow --print0 --monitor=poll_monitor | xargs -0 -I{} --no-run-if-empty /bin/sh -c 'export ACTUAL_PWD=${PWD}'
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:
```
Line 3	SC2016: Expressions don't expand in single quotes, use double quotes for that.
```
#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Expressions get expanded in the fork of `sh`, hence single quotes.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/1885701?v=4" width="50">[dimo414](https://github.com/dimo414) commented at [2021-11-22 20:29](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1894#issuecomment-975893599):

IMO this finding is correct, and should be [suppressed](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC2016#exceptions) if no variable expansion is intended. Callers could just as easily _want_ eager variable expansion, and single-quotes + a suppression makes it clear the author intended to lazily evaluate the variables in the subprocess' environment.

There is an [allowlist](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/blob/08d2eef4111afde4e24a0c7860ce9d8725db37a6/src/ShellCheck/Analytics.hs#L805) for commands that often expect single-quoted variables, but I worry `xargs` would be too heavy-handed a command to include in that list.
