#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/3129206?v=4" width="50">[matthewpersico](https://github.com/matthewpersico) commented at [2018-05-10T01:26:16Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1213#issuecomment-387922558):

The statement `((5 > aarray[bar]))`, is not doing what you think it is.
I created another script to demonstrate. See this and its output:

```
#!/bin/bash
declare bar=a 
declare -A aarray=([a]=6)
echo "${aarray[a]}"
if ((5 > aarray[bar]))
then
echo "true to 5 > ${aarray[$bar]}"
else 
echo "false to 5 > ${aarray[$bar]}"
fi
if ((5 > aarray[$bar]))
then
echo "true to 5 > ${aarray[$bar]}"
else
echo "false to 5 > ${aarray[$bar]}"
fi
```
```
./scissue.1213
6
true to 5 > 6
false to 5 > 6
```
I think that the statement `aarray[bar]` doesn't work in (()). You can drop the $ on the associative array, but not the index.
