#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

The fact that I have `-o pipefail` defined means anything in a pipe chain *will* cause an error and is *not* masked as the the `SC2312` check indicates. #### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-11-11 05:25](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2368#issuecomment-966000993):

ShellCheck is right, pipefail does not affect process substitutions. In something like `cat <(exit 1)`, the failure in the process substitution is quietly suppressed.

If you invoke it separately, e.g. with

```
env | grep -E "^(USER|HOME|PATH|TERM)" | sort > tmp
while IFS='' read -r line; do array+=("${line}"); done < tmp
```

then `set -e` will trigger if `env` fails, and you also won't get a warning from the optional `check-extra-masked-returns`

PS: `enable=all` is not like `-Wall`. It's really meant for discovery, and may enable subjective or mutually conflicting checks.
