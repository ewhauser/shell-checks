I would like to generalize this request as "simplify adding support for other Bourne shell flavors". Developers of embedded systems (including me) often have to deal with scripts written for built-in shell of busybox, which has various extensions above POSIX, but far not everything that bash supports, and set of available extensions may vary depending on version.

I think it makes sense to add some kind of "shell profile" text file which lists what kinds of syntactic constructions should not produce warnings. I hope same approach will be useful for supporting shells like pdksh and mksh as well.

@nihilus 

> How does the public domain implementation of ksh differs from ksh93?

"ksh" can potentially be installed as one of a variety of ksh derivatives, though today is most likely to be a ksh93 compliant shell such as mksh, pdksh, etc. Technically, "ksh" can even be a really old, non-POSIX shell, though today this is typically named lksh to distinguish between them. So it's just a matter of which particular ksh a system has installed.

Ah, I am reading the mksh FAQ as explaining a few things:

* A: mksh is not a superset of ksh and should probably not be parsed as such.
* B: In addition to mksh, many other ksh variants exist: ksh86, ksh88, ksh93, ksh2020, pdksh, MKS ksh, lksh (legacy ksh), dtksh, and SKsh.
* C: To a limited extent, POSIX sh, bash, and zsh are influenced by ksh. Not particularly actionable compared to A, B. But still good to know.

http://www.mirbsd.org/mksh-faq.htm#kornshell

Finally, pdksh is considered basically POSIX compatible. Many pdksh shebanged scripts could be checked similar to ksh scripts.

https://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Guide_to_Unix/Explanations/Choice_of_Shell
