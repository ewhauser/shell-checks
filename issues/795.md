#!/bin/sh

  sed : $file

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting. [SC2086]

-----

It seems like `shellcheck` tries to be clever and will look what you are iterating in the `for` loop. But doesn't seems to realize potential errors wih `for stuff`

Writing`for stuff` is a shorthand for writing `for stuff in "$@"`. Currently `for stuff in "$@"` works as expected (Throws warning about pathname expansion and word splitting) while `for stuff` doesn't (No warning).
