# [\#203 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/203) `closed`: Encourage 'single' quotes over "double" quotes

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/5316?v=4" width="50">[mcandre](https://github.com/mcandre) opened issue at [2014-08-06T01:31:25Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/203):

"Double" quotes may add a certain amount of complexity / slowdown, such as interpolation and escape characters. 'Single' quotes are preferred for simple strings.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2014-08-08T16:46:26Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/203#issuecomment-51627807):

I see your point, but this is really more of a stylistic choice and I don't think ShellCheck should be making it.


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/5316?v=4" width="50">[mcandre](https://github.com/mcandre) commented at [2014-08-08T17:11:13Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/203#issuecomment-51630609):

I realize that not all users would like to apply this rule, and there's no reason to force them to. What if this were an optional rule, turned off by default?


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2014-08-09T16:20:15Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/203#issuecomment-51691273):
