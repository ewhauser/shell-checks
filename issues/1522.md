#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2019-03-18T01:37:11Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1522#issuecomment-473739751):

This happens because your `ps` command does not just output e.g. `1` but instead <code>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1</code> (with a pile of whitespace).

One way to fix this for the quoted case is to ask `ps` not to pad by setting a low field width:

    parent_window="$(ps --no-headers -p "$(ps --no-headers -o ppid:1= $PPID)" -o comm=)"

It's not really a false positive since you do in fact accidentally lose the whitespace around the output, but since you expect exactly one number surrounded by whitespace you don't care about, you can definitely justify ignoring it.
