personally I've adopted something along the lines of; 

``` bash
private_storage () {
    if [[ -z "${HALCYON_AWS_ACCESS_KEY_ID:+_}"
       || -z "${HALCYON_AWS_SECRET_ACCESS_KEY:+_}"
       || -z "${HALCYON_S3_BUCKET:+_}"
       || -z "${HALCYON_S3_ACL:+_}" ]]
    then
        return 1
    fi
}
```

I've tried to figure out experimentally where bash considers a linefeed appropriate or inappropriate in [[ ]], but the logic wasn't worth it and collided with zsh. As of e909c8a, shellcheck should in most cases just skip them in [[ ]] and warn about them in [ ], even when bash complains (like in `[[ foo \n ]]`).
