#!/usr/bin/env bash
if [ "${SKIP_TERM_MODE}" -eq 0 ] ; then

#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

SC2154 for `SKIP_TERM_MODE`.

I know that capital variables have this shady status of sometimes being considered as "provided by the context". However, I'd propose a shellcheck flag, or a mode (`set -Eeuo pipefail`) that would propose assigning defaults `${SKIP_TERM_MODE:-}`.

Bonus points: In this case, a numerical value.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2020-07-26 00:13](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2019#issuecomment-663919228):

Have a look at the `check-unassigned-uppercase` optional check from `shellcheck --list-optional`:

```sh
name:    check-unassigned-uppercase
desc:    Warn when uppercase variables are unassigned
example: echo $VAR
fix:     VAR=hello; echo $VAR
```

It does exactly this, and you can enable it with `shellcheck -o check-unassigned-uppercase ..` or `# shellcheck enable=check-unassigned-uppercase`
