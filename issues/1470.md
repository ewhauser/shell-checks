#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2019-01-21T05:55:54Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1470#issuecomment-455956630):

@austin987 I think that this may be related to the shell's path canonicalization more than your actual ability to `cd`.

`cd ..` in `/` is fine, it just leads you back to `/`. I think it'll only ever fail if the directory is removed or if you `chmod -x ..` or similar after going there.

I love the technical correctness of this suggestion. Pragmatically though, I think it's fair to assume that `cd ..` is sufficiently unlikely to fail that any warning is more noise than signal, and the warning for `cd ..` is explicitly and intentionally squashed.  `cd .` warning is a plain oversight, as is `cd ../..`. This has been fixed in e8f913e9. Additionally, 1d60a4a4 makes shellcheck say `pushd` instead of `cd` when that's the command you're using.

Thanks for the suggestion!
