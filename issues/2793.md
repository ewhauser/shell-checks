#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2023-07-31 04:33](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2793#issuecomment-1657549271):

"Useless Use Of Cat" is/was a meme in the shell scripting community, predating ShellCheck by decades. The warning is about 80% nod to tradition and 20% helpful advice.

While classic UUOC happens all the time organically, it's much more rare for anyone to unintentionally append `| cat` to anything. If they do, it's much more likely to be for semi-legitimate reasons like making stdout not a tty for formatting and buffering reasons.

Looking across a corpus, the most common case (which is still quite rare compared to `cat file | ..`) is to have a `cat` seemingly added to make a tool stop outputting in color, but which was subsequently made redundant by redirecting or capturing the output. This would be a nifty thing to point out, but it's ultimately very low impact, so it's likely not worth the complexity.
