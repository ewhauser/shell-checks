#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/3999221?v=4" width="50">[pawamoy](https://github.com/pawamoy) commented at [2018-07-23T13:59:23Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1301#issuecomment-407067571):

So you mean you want to match `rom` **or** `controller` **or** `description` **or** `=`, and not the concatenated `rom|controller|description|=` string? If yes, you should write it with simple-quotes and no backslash: `grep 'rom|controller|description|='`. 

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-07-23T23:19:04Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1301#issuecomment-407230564):

Here's the wiki page with additional information: https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC1117

ShellCheck is right.  It's unlikely to cause problems, but `"\|"` is indeed an unknown escape sequence in double quoted bash strings. `grep 'rom\|controller'` and `grep "rom\\|controller"` do the same thing without relying on unknown escape sequences.

Alternatively, `grep -E 'rom|controller'` does the same thing in a POSIX portable way.
