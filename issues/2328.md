#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-09-26 01:55](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2328#issuecomment-927213235):

You're right, ideally such cases would be caught. In this particular case it requires modelling `find`'s behavior and realizing that being invoked with a path `/` and no further predicates will cause the -exec to run with `{}` to be substituted for `/` and `/usr` and several other directories that probably shouldn't be removed. 

This type of symbolic execution is unfortunately beyond ShellCheck at this point, and likely always will be.

The `rm -rf /` warning does not aim to prove that a script is safe and won't delete files, but much less ambitiously to help detect typo accidents like the infamous `rm -rf /usr /lib/nvidia-current/xorg/xorg` case.
