#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-11-25T04:47:04Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1387#issuecomment-441415177):

You're right. This is a quirky bash specific behavior, where words that look like assignments are parsed as such and therefore allow parentheses where they normally wouldn't be valid. It's a bit iffy to recreate, so for now I've just updated [the wiki](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/wiki/SC1036).

tl;dr: Use the equivalent command `eval "declare -A assocArray=($myString)"`
