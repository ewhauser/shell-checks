#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/708570?v=4" width="50">[mmlb](https://github.com/mmlb) commented at [2018-05-10T01:52:32Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1212#issuecomment-387926873):

right, I think the issue is that shellcheck is incorrectly thinking that [] is array dereference, when really its just open/close square bracket literals.

I'm expecting shellcheck to _know_ that maker is not an array, but that just may be obvious in my obvious case and shellcheck has no way to assert that to be the case.

But I just went and checked if there's something in the wiki for this entry and even there it says that `$foo[bar]` is treated as `$foo` + `[bar]` so why is that not the same case for `$foo[]`. shellcheck.net is also erroring for the "compatibility" case in the wiki so I think something needs fixing. Either the wiki should be updated or this is classified as a regression in shellcheck and subsequently fixed.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2018-05-13T00:14:28Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1212#issuecomment-388592081):

Shellcheck was incorrectly thinking that maybe *you* thought it would be an array reference.

Perl and PHP both use `$foo[bar]` to access elements in an array, so this is not always an obvious mistake.

I agree that the wiki worded this strangely, and I've updated it. Thanks!
