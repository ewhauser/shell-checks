Version 0.7.0

- [x] The rule's wiki page does not already cover this
- [x] I tried on shellcheck.net and verified that this is still a problem on the latest commit

#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh

#!/bin/bash
mlr clean-whitespace then sort -f group foo.csv
```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:
`^-- SC1010: Use semicolon or linefeed before 'then' (or quote to make it literal).`


#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Nothing as `then` is a [`mlr`](https://johnkerl.org/miller/doc/) keyword.

While @afranke 's reasoning is incorrect, his example really does point out a shellcheck bug. Why is shellcheck treating `then` as a reserved word without a preceding `if`? The `bash` man page clearly states:

> *RESERVED WORDS*
>
> The following words are recognized as reserved when unquoted and either the first word of a simple command (see SHELL GRAMMAR below) or the third word of a `case` or `for` command:

`then` in the OP's example doesn't meet either of the above conditions, and therefore shouldn't be treated as special.
