#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2015-10-03T23:29:28Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/489#issuecomment-145299029):

I don't know of any specific systems in which this fails, but according to[ POSIX `test`](http://pubs.opengroup.org/onlinepubs/9699919799/utilities/test.html):

```
>4 arguments:
    The results are unspecified.
```


#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/1042559?v=4" width="50">[tr00per](https://github.com/tr00per) commented at [2015-10-04T11:06:47Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/489#issuecomment-145336893):

GNU coreutils manual doesn't mention any unspecified behavior for `test`: www.gnu.org/software/coreutils/manual/coreutils.html
That's what major distributions use, so the SC2166 advice is useless in practice.

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/302055?v=4" width="50">[deryni](https://github.com/deryni) commented at [2015-10-04T15:35:50Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/489#issuecomment-145359004):

The coreutils don't document the parsing at all which is the stage at which POSIX indicates the unspecified behavior. Also what coreutils does (or does not do) at the moment isn't really relevant to what the spec says is required (or rather what is specified) so yes, warning about spec incompliance is absolutely worthwhile to my mind.
