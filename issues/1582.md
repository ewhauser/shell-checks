# [\#1582 Issue](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1582) `closed`: SC2086 caught on quoting a variable inside a "$(...)"

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2657382?v=4" width="50">[salderma](https://github.com/salderma) opened issue at [2019-05-15T20:43:03Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1582):


- SC2086
- 0.3.5 from EL7 EPEL

```sh
In myscript.sh line 50:
      [ -w "$(dirname ${outputf})" ] || die "$0: Permision denied writing ${outputf}"
                      ^-- SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.


In myscript.sh line 57:
      [ -w "$(dirname \"${outputf}\")" ] || die "$0: Permission denied writing ${outputf}"
                        ^-- SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.

```

These two lines check write access to an output file.  I have tried multiple quoting scenarios and can't get this to resolve.  Two of them are shown above, a third would be like:
```sh
in myscript.sh line 57:
      [ -w $(dirname "${outputf}") ] || die "$0: Permision denied writing ${outputf}"
                      ^-- SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.
```
What should I do?

#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2014764?v=4" width="50">[kpankonen](https://github.com/kpankonen) commented at [2019-05-16T00:19:58Z](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/1582#issuecomment-492869845):

```
[ -w "$(dirname "${outputf}")" ] || die "$0: Permision denied writing ${outputf}
```

Sheesh, I must have had bad luck trying this on my command prompt, because the quoting wasn't nesting right.  Whatever I did, lead me to the conclusion that bash saw quoted strings as `"$(dirname "` and `")"`.

Anyway, thanks that works.
