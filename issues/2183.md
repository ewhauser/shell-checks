#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/824177?v=4" width="50">[JoshuaSCochrane](https://github.com/JoshuaSCochrane) commented at [2021-03-22 20:31](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2183#issuecomment-804373464):

Oh, and maybe suggest `${1:-1}` (default-value) here instead, which is what I _meant_ to use ;)

From https://www.gnu.org/savannah-checkouts/gnu/bash/manual/bash.html#Shell-Parameter-Expansion:

> `${parameter:=word}`
If _parameter_ is unset or null, the expansion of _word_ is assigned to _parameter_. The value of _parameter_ is then substituted. Positional parameters and special parameters may not be assigned to in this way.

So it must apply to 'special parameters' as well, whatever those are.
