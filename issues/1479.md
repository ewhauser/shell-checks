#### Here's a snippet or screenshot that shows the problem:

```sh
#!/usr/bin/env bash

# init-nfsmachine - repopulate known_hosts interactively

for i in $(seq 1 8)
do
    ssh foo${i}.bar.com "echo 'Connected!'"
done

```

#### Here's what shellcheck currently says:
```
Line 7	SC2086: Double quote to prevent globbing and word splitting.
```
#### Here's what I wanted or expected to see:

Nothing. `seq` prints numbers separated by newlines. Each number cannot contain spaces, therefore this `i` does not need quotes. 

If the shellcheck analysis cannot determine that the `i`, unassigned between its initial assignment and this usage, will not contain spaces. then feel free to close.
