#### <img src="https://avatars.githubusercontent.com/u/2818758?u=40d36e49d938fda80abd46d6347b9bace1f73dd4&v=4" width="50">[koalaman](https://github.com/koalaman) commented at [2021-08-27 02:00](https://github.com/koalaman/shellcheck/issues/2209#issuecomment-906866824):

The list is not very logical, no. It's part empirical, with commands chosen because they occur with some frequency, and part strategic, to encourage contributions. It's not meant to be an exhaustive list by any means, but I added `rg` to it.

`sed` can't be excluded universally because `sed -e 's/$search/$replace/g'` is an especially common mistake that ShellCheck really should catch. However, including it universally would be pretty annoying as well due to the many `sed '$aFoo'` type expressions. Meanwhile, it's much less common to do something like `awk '/$name/'`. It's heuristics all the way down.

The intent of the warning is to help newbies who think single and double quotes are interchangable like in JS or Python. Please don't hesitate to perma-disable it in your `.shellcheckrc`.
